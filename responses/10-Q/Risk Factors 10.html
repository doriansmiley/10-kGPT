Source: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1321655/000132165522000032/pltr-20220930.htm

Rating: Negative, Score: Below Average The extracted information highlights several risks related to potential business acquisitions, investments, and data security. Acquiring companies may pose risks if they operate in unfamiliar markets or have prior non-compliance issues. Cash payments or debt incurred in these transactions may also limit the company's flexibility. Additionally, investments in companies with unproven technologies or services may decrease earnings per share and negatively impact revenue and collections. Furthermore, the risks and costs associated with data security breaches and cyber-attacks are significant and can result in the loss of business, reduced revenue, liability claims, harm to reputation or competitive position, and financial losses. The risks and vulnerabilities of relying on third-party vendors, whose systems and networks may contain exploitable defects, bugs, and security vulnerabilities, further increase these potential negative impacts. Overall, these risks may have a material adverse effect on the company's business, financial position, and future performance, warranting increased diligence from a prospective investor.
