Rating: Negative, Score: Below Average Key Points: - The company has a multi-class share structure where the Founders and their affiliates hold a significant amount of Class F common stock which provides them with effective control over the company. - The Ownership Threshold that must be met on the applicable record date is a small minority of the outstanding Corporation Equity Securities that may decrease further with future issuances of equity. - The Founders and their affiliates can freely transfer or dispose of their shares of Class A and Class B common stock without diminishing their voting power, as long as they meet the Ownership Threshold on the applicable record date. - The Ownership Threshold could be reduced on a pro-rata basis upon the withdrawal or removal of one or more of the Founders from the Founder Voting Agreement, which may lead to increased voting power of the remaining Founders. - The company can create a new class of equity securities with different economic or voting rights than existing classes which could increase the divergence between the economic interests and voting power of the Founders and their affiliates. Overall, the company's multi-class share structure and ownership arrangement may not be favorable to potential investors as it concentrates voting power in the hands of the Founders and their affiliates, potentially limiting the influence of outside investors.
