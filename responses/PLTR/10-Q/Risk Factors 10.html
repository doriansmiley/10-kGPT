Source: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1321655/000132165522000032/pltr-20220930.htm

Rating: Negative, Score: Below Average The 10-Q and 10-K filings contain information that may be negative for a potential investor. The company may acquire companies with little or no prior experience or may have to comply with additional laws and regulations. If the company incurs debt to fund acquisitions, they may be subject to material restrictions on their ability to conduct business along with financial maintenance covenants. Existing stockholders may experience dilution, and earnings per share may decrease if equity securities are issued in connection with such transactions. The company has made strategic investments and entered into commercial contracts with Investees, and if they are unable to generate sufficient revenues or profitability, the company's expected revenue and collections would be negatively impacted. Furthermore, the company's ability to sell or transfer or realize value from investments may be limited by securities laws and regulations. There are also risks related to intellectual property, information technology, data privacy, and security, and public perception of the company may be harmed if unauthorized access to customer data is obtained. They rely on third-party products and components that have been subject to significant attacks. There is a risk of cybersecurity attacks and threats that could materially disrupt the company's systems and operations, supply chain, and ability to produce, sell, and distribute products and services. Despite the implementation of network security measures and internal information security policies, data stored on personnel computer systems is also at risk.
