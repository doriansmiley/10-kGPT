The company's innovative data cloud platform, which consolidates data into a single source of truth, is being adopted by organizations of all sizes across a broad range of industries. The company's substantial customer base includes many of the world's largest organizations, and the customer count has increased to 7,828 as of January 31, 2023, from 5,967 as of January 31, 2022. Snowflake's revenue primarily comes from fees charged to customers based on the compute, storage, and data transfer resources consumed on its platform, and its deferred revenue is not a meaningful indicator of future revenue recognized. Furthermore, Snowflake's business model is consumption-based, which means the company only charges customers for the resources they use. Additionally, Snowflake's net revenue retention rate is 158% and 177% as of January 31, 2023, and 2022, respectively, indicating strong customer loyalty. However, Snowflake is monitoring the effects of such macroeconomic conditions on its business.
