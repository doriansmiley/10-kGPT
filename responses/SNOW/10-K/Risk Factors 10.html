Source: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1640147/000164014723000030/snow-20230131.htm

Rating: Negative Score: Below Average Summary: - Compliance with evolving laws, regulations, and standards poses a risk to the company's business if they fail to comply - Being a public company requires substantial expenses and management efforts to establish proper internal control over financial reporting, failure to maintain adequate control may adversely affect the company's financial reports accuracy, investor confidence, and future access to capital markets - Anti-takeover provisions in charter documents and Delaware law may limit attempts to replace or remove current management, restrict potential acquirers, and reduce the likelihood of stockholders receiving a premium for their shares - Exclusive forum provisions in amended and restated certificate of incorporation will restrict stockholders' ability to choose the judicial forum for disputes, may lead to significant additional costs associated with resolving such actions in other jurisdictions, and harm the company's business.
