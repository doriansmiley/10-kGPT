Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations 0

Source: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1640147/000164014722000100/snow-20221031.htm

Rating: Positive, Score: Above Average Summary: - Snowflake is delivering the Data Cloud, which allows for seamless access to data sets in secure, governed, and compliant ways. - Its cloud-native architecture is built on three major public clouds across 37 regional deployments around the world, enabling a consistent, global user experience. - The substantial majority of its revenue comes from fees charged to customers based on the storage, compute, and data transfer resources consumed on its platform, with consumption typically accelerating from the beginning of the customers' usage to the end of their contract terms. - Snowflake focuses on acquiring new customers and increasing the migration of additional customer workloads to drive increased consumption, as evidenced by its net revenue retention rate of 165% and 177% as of October 31, 2022 and January 31, 2022, respectively. - Snowflake had 7,292 total customers as of October 31, 2022, increasing from 5,966 customers as of January 31, 2022, and its platform is used globally by organizations of all sizes across a broad range of industries. - A recent business combination of Streamlit, Inc. for $650.8 million is expected to provide further growth. - Snowflake has been impacted by adverse macroeconomic conditions and the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, but it has responded well by requiring virtually all of its employees to work remotely and adjusting marketing events to be virtual or include both an in-person and virtual experience. Overall, Snowflake's business model, platform, and focus on customer acquisition and increased consumption provide good growth potential, although there may be some risks related to macroeconomic conditions and the ongoing pandemic.
